Understanding the Results

Each concept below is explained using your actual results — not textbook examples.

What is a market regime?

Markets don't move in straight lines. They cycle between periods of calm expansion — where trend-following works — and periods of fear and dislocation where holding through losses destroys wealth.

AMCE classifies every week into one of three states using five independent signals: momentum (are prices trending up?), structure (is price above its long-term average?), fear (what is the VIX telling us?), and fundamentals (what is the yield curve saying about the economy?).

When three or more signals agree, the model takes a full position. When only two agree, it takes a half position. When one or zero agree, it steps to safety.

Key Insight
Why 5 rules instead of 400?

The most common mistake in quantitative finance is overfitting — building a model so precisely tuned to historical data that it captures noise instead of signal. A strategy with 50 parameters, each optimized on the same dataset it will be tested on, can show spectacular backtested performance and fail completely in live trading.

AMCE uses five signals, each with thresholds set by academic research or platform defaults, not optimization:

• 200-day MA threshold: Faber (2007), "A Quantitative Approach to Tactical Asset Allocation"
• 12-1 month momentum: Jegadeesh & Titman (1993), replicated across 40+ markets
• VIX = 25 threshold: Standard institutional risk-management cutoff, CBOE +1 s.d.
• Yield curve inversion: Fed research, 1960s–present predictive record
• Supertrend (ATR 5, factor 2): volatility-adjusted trend filter — tighter bands react faster to regime shifts

Key Insight
How does this compare to a hedge fund?

Institutional performance is measured by Sharpe ratio — the return per unit of risk taken. A Sharpe above 0.5 is considered good. Above 1.0 is considered excellent. Most hedge funds target 0.8–1.2 after fees.

The 60/40 portfolio — 60% stocks, 40% bonds — is the standard benchmark for moderate-risk investing. It has historically achieved a Sharpe of approximately 0.45–0.55.

Key Insight
What does this cost to run?

Every trade has a cost. Bid-ask spreads, commissions, and market impact eat into returns. High-frequency strategies can lose most of their alpha to transaction costs. AMCE is designed to trade infrequently.
